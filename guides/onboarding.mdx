---
title: "Onboarding a New Client for Document Identity Verification"
sidebarTitle: "Client Onboarding"
description: "Walk through Quick KYC's onboarding wizard to enroll a new client, upload document scans, pass quality checks, and trigger OCR verification."
---

Quick KYC's onboarding wizard guides you through every step required to enroll a new client and submit their identity document for on-premises verification. Each stage is enforced sequentially — you cannot advance until the current step passes — so that only high-quality, tamper-free submissions reach the OCR pipeline. The sections below walk you through the full flow from initial data entry to background processing.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Enter Client Details">
    Fill in the client's personal information exactly as it appears on their physical document. Quick KYC cross-references every value you enter here against the OCR-extracted fields during the audit stage, so precision matters.

    | Field | Notes |
    |---|---|
    | **Full Name** | As printed on the document — no abbreviations |
    | **Email Address** | Used for notifications and record lookup |
    | **Date of Birth** | DD / MM / YYYY format |
    | **Document Serial Number** | Passport book number, ID number, or licence number |
    | **Document Type** | Passport, National ID, or Driver's License |
    | **Country of Issuance** | ISO country of the issuing authority |

    <Note>
      The demographics you enter here serve as the ground-truth reference. During OCR processing, any field where the extracted value diverges from your input will be flagged with a confidence-drop indicator in the audit portal.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose an Ingestion Profile">
    Select the ingestion profile that matches your operational context. The profile determines which document image is fed into the quality and OCR pipeline.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Clean Profile">
        Uses a standard, clean sample document — for example, a Canadian Passport — with no forensic anomalies. Risk scores will be low, making this profile ideal for:

        - Onboarding pipeline smoke tests
        - Operator training sessions
        - Integration validation in staging environments
      </Tab>

      <Tab title="Suspicious Profile">
        Uses a sample document seeded with simulated fraud signatures — for example, an expired national ID with geometry deviations. This profile is designed to:

        - Trigger the compliance review workflow end-to-end
        - Validate that risk signals surface correctly in the audit portal
        - Test your team's manual-review response procedures
      </Tab>

      <Tab title="Custom Scan">
        Upload a real image file from your workstation or capture a live snapshot using your device's webcam. Use this profile for all genuine client verifications. Accepted formats are JPEG, PNG, and HEIC at a minimum resolution of 800 × 600 px.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload Document Scans">
    Provide one or more image captures of the client's document. Quick KYC accepts the following scan surfaces:

    <CardGroup cols={3}>
      <Card title="Front Face" icon="id-card">
        **Required for all document types.** Must show the photo, full name, document number, and expiry date clearly and without obstruction.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Back Face" icon="rotate">
        **Optional for passports; recommended for National IDs and Driver's Licenses.** Captures the machine-readable zone (MRZ) or barcode on reverse-side documents.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Selfie" icon="camera">
        **Optional — enables biometric check.** A live or uploaded portrait photo used to compute a facial similarity score against the document photograph.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>

    Position the document on a flat, matte surface under even lighting before capturing. Avoid placing it on screens or reflective backgrounds.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Quality Analysis">
    After upload, the **Capture Quality Intelligence Engine** analyses each image automatically before you can proceed to submission. Review the three quality signals reported on screen:

    | Signal | What It Measures | Fail Condition |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Sharpness Score** | Overall image clarity and focus | Score below acceptable threshold |
    | **Shadow Detection** | Uneven lighting that may obscure holograms or text | Severe shadow coverage detected |
    | **Screen Recapture Detection** | Whether the image is a photograph of a digital screen rather than a physical document | Screen display pattern detected |

    <Warning>
      If screen recapture or severe blur is detected, the **Submit** button is disabled and you will not be able to proceed. You must re-capture the physical document directly — photographing a screen showing someone else's document image is blocked at the platform level and constitutes a compliance violation. Re-take the scan under proper conditions and re-upload before continuing.
    </Warning>

    When all three signals pass, a green **Quality Approved** banner appears and the **Submit for Verification** button becomes active.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit for OCR Verification">
    Click **Submit for Verification** to hand off the record to Quick KYC's on-premises verification engine. The system will extract all PII fields from the document, cross-reference them against your initial client-detail inputs, evaluate forensic risk signals, and assign an initial status — all without any data leaving your infrastructure.

    The record is immediately written to the **Ingestion Queue** in the compliance dashboard with a status of **`PENDING`**. Processing takes place entirely on your local nodes — no data leaves your infrastructure.
  </Step>

</Steps>

<Note>
  After submission, Quick KYC processes the document asynchronously in the background. You do not need to keep the onboarding wizard open. The record will appear in the **Ingestion Queue** on the compliance dashboard, and its status will transition automatically from `PENDING` to either `UNDER_REVIEW` (if risk signals require human inspection) or `APPROVED` (if all checks pass within configured thresholds). You will see the updated status the next time you load or refresh the dashboard queue.
</Note>
